# 1.1.1 Organizational Scope And Boundaries

_Page Type: Concept Explainer | Maturity: Draft_

The atlas is written for organizations of different sizes and sectors that need to apply AI and ML under real delivery, legal, operational, and sourcing constraints.

This file matters because the chapter on scope and principles depends on readers being able to move from broad chapter framing to a sharper local distinction, journey, or scope rule. The atlas works only when even small files carry enough narrative framing to tell the reader why the material exists and what it should change in practice.

## Why This File Matters

The point here is not length for its own sake. The point is to make the surrounding chapter more usable by adding explanation, selective bullets, and a more explicit reading posture around the existing material.

## Included Organizational Contexts

- Business and enterprise settings, including internal productivity, customer-facing systems, and platform programs.
- Public-sector and regulated environments where transparency, evidence, auditability, and procurement discipline matter early.
- Nonprofit and mission-driven organizations that still face lock-in, privacy, staffing, and platform concentration risks.
- Small and medium organizations that need practical decision support without pretending they have hyperscaler-scale platform teams.

## Scope Boundaries

The atlas covers implementation, governance, architecture, sourcing, and operating questions around applied AI and ML. It does not try to be a pure research repository, a vendor ranking site, or a legal advice substitute.

## What Is Out Of Scope

- generic AI hype or trend commentary without implementation consequence
- consumer-only product reviews disconnected from organizational use
- legal advice framed as if it removed the need for internal counsel or regulatory interpretation
- model benchmarks presented without architecture, governance, or deployment context

## What To Carry Forward

- Use this file to sharpen decisions, not just to restate the chapter title in smaller pieces.
- Keep the chapter's cross-cutting priorities visible when applying the local guidance.
- Revisit the chapter front door if this file starts to feel more detailed than the decision really needs.

## Practical Reading Rule

Read this file as a local clarifier inside the chapter. If it changes nothing about the decision, review, or reading path in front of you, the reader is probably in the wrong place.
