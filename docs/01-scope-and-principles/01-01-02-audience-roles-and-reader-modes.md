# 1.1.2 Audience, Roles, And Reader Modes

_Page Type: Concept Explainer | Maturity: Draft_

The atlas is meant for mixed teams. No single role sees the whole system, so the repository is organized to help different readers converge on the same decision from different entry points.

This file matters because the chapter on scope and principles depends on readers being able to move from broad chapter framing to a sharper local distinction, journey, or scope rule. The atlas works only when even small files carry enough narrative framing to tell the reader why the material exists and what it should change in practice.

## Why This File Matters

The point here is not length for its own sake. The point is to make the surrounding chapter more usable by adding explanation, selective bullets, and a more explicit reading posture around the existing material.

## Role Coverage

- Product, project, and business roles: product managers, project managers, business analysts, domain experts.
- Architecture and design roles: enterprise architects, AI architects, software architects.
- Build roles: developers, ML engineers, data engineers, platform engineers.
- Assurance and control roles: QA and test engineers, security professionals, privacy professionals, governance, risk, and compliance professionals.
- Operate and source roles: IT operations, platform operations, procurement and sourcing roles.
- Decision-making roles: technical leadership and organizational decision-makers.

## Reader Modes

- Orientation mode: understand the landscape, vocabulary, and adjacent concerns.
- Implementation mode: design or review an actual system, control set, or sourcing choice.
- Assurance mode: evaluate privacy, security, quality, and compliance implications.
- Leadership mode: decide how much control, openness, sovereignty, and platform dependence the organization wants to tolerate.

## Reading Heuristic

If the team is arguing about tools before it agrees on audience, system type, risk posture, and operating ownership, it is usually early. Start with this chapter, then move to chapters `2`, `3`, `4`, `5`, and `6` before settling the implementation layer.

## What To Carry Forward

- Use this file to sharpen decisions, not just to restate the chapter title in smaller pieces.
- Keep the chapter's cross-cutting priorities visible when applying the local guidance.
- Revisit the chapter front door if this file starts to feel more detailed than the decision really needs.

## Practical Reading Rule

Read this file as a local clarifier inside the chapter. If it changes nothing about the decision, review, or reading path in front of you, the reader is probably in the wrong place.
