# 16.1 Oversight Foundations

_Page Type: Chapter Index | Maturity: Draft_

This section defines what counts as real human oversight before teams debate tooling, standards, or operating details. Use it to name accountable owners, approval rights, escalation lanes, and stop authority so "human in the loop" does not hide unclear responsibility or ceremonial review.

## Section Map

- 16.1.2 [Decision Boundaries And Governance Heuristics](16-01-02-decision-boundaries-and-governance-heuristics.md)

## What This Section Helps Decide

- who owns the service outcome versus who approves cases, operates queues, sets policy baselines, or supplies managed controls
- which actions stay advisory, which require bounded human approval, and which should remain outside automation until the operating model is redesigned
- what evidence must exist before oversight claims are credible, including approval packets, escalation routes, durable records, and intervention rights
- when the real issue has shifted into governance, gateway enforcement, observability, security, or sourcing rather than oversight vocabulary alone
