# 16.3.1 Standards And Bodies

_Page Type: Comparison Page | Maturity: Draft_

This page compares the external anchors that matter once the oversight lane, decision rights, and intervention burden are already clear. Read it as an oversight crosswalk: first decide whether the gap is legal accountability, management-system design, case-level human review, or operating-model implementation, then choose the smallest credible starting set instead of treating every named framework as one interchangeable "human in the loop" checklist.

| Standards tracking and implementation planning | AI Pact + ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42 + NIST ITL AI Program | gives teams a compact way to follow external movement without pretending standards tracking is the same thing as operating control | `20`, `21` |

## Reading Rule

- Use laws to identify where human review, accountability, or contestability obligations exist.
- Use standards and frameworks to structure oversight ownership, impact review, and operating-model design.
- Use programs and committees to track external movement, not to replace local decision-rights matrices, approval thresholds, or intervention drills.
