# 12.3 Reference Points

_Page Type: Chapter Index | Maturity: Review-Ready_

This section collects the named comparison material that matters after the adaptation path is already framed. Use it to compare prompt-optimization, fine-tuning, and training platforms without collapsing the chapter back into a tool ranking exercise or skipping the evidence needed to justify escalation.

## Included Reference Subsections

- 12.3.1 [Tools And Platforms](12-03-01-tools-and-platforms.md)

## What This Section Helps Decide

- which tools are meant to keep work in prompt or program space rather than push teams prematurely into weight updates
- when self-managed adaptation stacks are realistic and when managed platforms better fit staffing, control, or delivery constraints
- where openness, portability, data handling, and estate dependence materially change the adaptation choice
- when a named-tool question is really a retrieval, hosting, evaluation, or sourcing question instead of an adaptation-method question

## Reading Boundaries

- Use [12.3.1 Tools And Platforms](12-03-01-tools-and-platforms.md) to compare adaptation tooling by stack role, control posture, and lock-in profile.
- Revisit [11. Knowledge Retrieval And Memory](../11-knowledge-retrieval-and-memory/11-00-00-knowledge-retrieval-and-memory.md) when the real fix is knowledge access, grounding quality, or memory design rather than parameter changes.
- Revisit [8. Model Hosting And Inference](../08-model-hosting-and-inference/08-00-00-model-hosting-and-inference.md) when GPU capacity, serving topology, or runtime ownership is the binding constraint.
- Revisit [13. Evaluation Testing And QA](../13-evaluation-testing-and-qa/13-00-00-evaluation-testing-and-qa.md) when teams cannot yet prove that a fine-tuning or training step improves the system enough to justify its added lifecycle burden.
- Revisit [18. Build Vs Buy Vs Hybrid](../18-build-vs-buy-vs-hybrid/18-00-00-build-vs-buy-vs-hybrid.md) when the dispute is really about platform dependence, sourcing posture, or whether adaptation should be an internal capability at all.
