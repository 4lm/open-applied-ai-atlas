# 6.3.1 Standards And Bodies

This subsection gathers the main privacy and sovereignty reference points. The goal is to show which items answer legal questions, which support privacy engineering, and which clarify operational control posture.

## Laws And Regulatory Anchors

| Anchor | Type | Why it matters here | Primary source |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [GDPR](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj) | Regulation | Personal-data obligations, transfers, lawful basis, retention, deletion, and rights | EUR-Lex |
| [EU Data Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2023/2854/oj) | Regulation | Access, sharing, portability, and ecosystem control posture | EUR-Lex |
| [Data Governance Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2022/868/oj) | Regulation | Trusted data sharing, intermediation, and controlled reuse of protected data | EUR-Lex |
| [EU AI Act](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj) | Regulation | Personal-data and fundamental-rights implications that interact with AI risk controls | EUR-Lex |

## Reading Heuristic

GDPR and the EU Data Act answer different questions. GDPR centers personal-data obligations. The Data Act and Data Governance Act sharpen access, sharing, portability, and data-intermediation questions. Sovereignty also depends on telemetry, provenance, supply-chain metadata, and operational exit posture, so privacy law alone is not enough.
