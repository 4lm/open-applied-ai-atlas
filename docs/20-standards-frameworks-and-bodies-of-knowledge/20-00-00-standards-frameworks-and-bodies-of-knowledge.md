# 20. Standards Frameworks And Bodies Of Knowledge

_Page Type: Chapter Index | Maturity: Review-Ready_

This chapter turns standards work into a decision system. Use it to separate legal obligations, management-system design, risk frameworks, technical specifications, and standards-tracking bodies before a team starts citing them as if they all do the same job.

Standards-rich programs often fail for a simple reason: they collect respected names but never translate them into owners, controls, evidence, or re-review triggers.

## What This Chapter Helps Decide

- whether the current question is really about law, governance design, technical practice, or standards tracking
- which standards or frameworks should be combined instead of overloaded
- which outputs must exist locally before a standards claim is credible
- when the real issue belongs in governance, privacy, evaluation, security, oversight, sourcing, or community chapters instead
