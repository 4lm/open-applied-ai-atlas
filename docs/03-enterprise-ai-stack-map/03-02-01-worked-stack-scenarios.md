# 3.2.1 Worked Stack Scenarios

This subsection turns the chapter into concrete organizational situations so the abstractions can be checked against real delivery contexts.

These examples turn the chapter into concrete organizational situations. They are deliberately short and comparative so the reader can see where the chapter changes a real decision.

## Internal knowledge assistant

A team maps the service across governance, retrieval, model, gateway, observability, and oversight layers before choosing vendors.

- What this example makes visible: gives the atlas spine by showing the layers, control points, and cross-cutting concerns that shape real organizational AI systems.
- What to watch for: If they skip the map, they usually over-focus on model quality and under-design controls.

## Regulated workflow automation

An enterprise identifies approval, logging, retrieval, and rollback layers before allowing action-capable execution.

- What this example makes visible: gives the atlas spine by showing the layers, control points, and cross-cutting concerns that shape real organizational AI systems.
- What to watch for: The main risk is discovering too late that the governance and runtime layers are misaligned.

## Sovereign private deployment

A program uses the stack map to test whether self-hosting really solves its boundary and support constraints.

- What this example makes visible: gives the atlas spine by showing the layers, control points, and cross-cutting concerns that shape real organizational AI systems.
- What to watch for: The main mistake is treating hosting as the only sovereignty question.

## Portfolio rationalization

A platform team uses the stack map to explain where duplicated tools sit and which shared layers could be standardized.

- What this example makes visible: gives the atlas spine by showing the layers, control points, and cross-cutting concerns that shape real organizational AI systems.
- What to watch for: Without the map, platform rationalization turns into tool politics instead of architecture reasoning.
