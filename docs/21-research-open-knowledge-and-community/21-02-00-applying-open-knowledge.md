# 21.2 Applying Open Knowledge

_Page Type: Chapter Index | Maturity: Draft_

This section turns external signals into delivery review. Use it when a paper, benchmark, open-source project, nonprofit guide, or community practice already looks promising and the remaining question is whether it deserves a pilot, shortlist slot, control update, or local rejection.

## Section Map

- 21.2.1 [Worked Research-To-Practice Scenarios](21-02-01-worked-research-to-practice-scenarios.md)
- 21.2.2 [Patterns And Anti-Patterns](21-02-02-patterns-and-anti-patterns.md)

## What This Section Helps Decide

- which operating posture fits the external signal in front of the team, including paper-led exploration, benchmark-driven shortlist work, community-project adoption, or public guidance that may reshape controls
- which outputs should exist before outside knowledge changes internal defaults, including local success criteria, maintainer and stewardship checks, rollback plans, refresh ownership, and evidence packets
- which recurring operating shapes turn open knowledge into reusable organizational learning versus cargo-cult adoption, prestige sourcing, benchmark theater, or unmanaged dependency drift
- which changes should reopen model, retrieval, adaptation, evaluation, security, sourcing, or standards review before rollout expands
