# 4.2.1 Worked Governance Scenarios

This subsection turns the chapter into concrete organizational situations so the abstractions can be checked against real delivery contexts.

These examples turn the chapter into concrete organizational situations. They are deliberately short and comparative so the reader can see where the chapter changes a real decision.

## Low-risk internal assistant review

A team uses lightweight governance to classify the use case, assign ownership, and define evidence before release.

- What this example makes visible: covers the management-system layer of applied AI: policy, accountability, evidence, and the translation of governance intent into technical and operational controls.
- What to watch for: The failure mode is assuming “internal only” means no governance is needed.

## High-risk advisory workflow

A regulated workflow gets stronger approval gates, traceability requirements, and periodic review.

- What this example makes visible: covers the management-system layer of applied AI: policy, accountability, evidence, and the translation of governance intent into technical and operational controls.
- What to watch for: The failure mode is keeping advisory systems outside governance because they do not execute actions directly.

## Vendor exception process

A team needs a strategically important but weakly open service and documents a controlled exception with compensating controls.

- What this example makes visible: covers the management-system layer of applied AI: policy, accountability, evidence, and the translation of governance intent into technical and operational controls.
- What to watch for: Without explicit exception handling, the service becomes an accidental default.

## Post-incident governance loop

An incident triggers not just a fix, but a governance review that updates controls and release requirements.

- What this example makes visible: covers the management-system layer of applied AI: policy, accountability, evidence, and the translation of governance intent into technical and operational controls.
- What to watch for: The failure mode is treating incidents as purely technical defects.
